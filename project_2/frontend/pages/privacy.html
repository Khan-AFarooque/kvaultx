01 Zero-Knowledge Encryption Architecture

KvaultX is engineered from the ground up on a strict Zero-Knowledge Architecture. This fundamental design principal ensures that your master password, vault items, passkeys, and secure notes remain completely private and accessible exclusively to you.

  • Client-Side Encryption: All sensitive vault entries are encrypted locally on your personal device using AES-256 GCM encryption before being transmitted across the network.
  • No Master Key Access: KvaultX servers store only encrypted ciphertext blobs. We do not store, possess, or have access to your unencrypted Master Password or cryptographic keys.
  • Impenetrable Storage: In the event of a database breach or law enforcement subpoena, unencrypted vault data cannot be provided because we possess no technical capability to decrypt it.

02 Information We Collect & Process

We practice strict data minimization. We collect only the essential technical data necessary to authenticate your account and maintain service integrity:

  • Account Credentials: Full Name and Primary Email Address, required for account creation, welcome verification, security notification dispatches, and OTP verification.
  • Master Password Hash: Stored strictly as a salted Bcrypt hash (10 salt rounds) used purely to verify your identity during login. Plain-text master passwords are never logged or stored.
  • Security Metadata: IP addresses, browser user-agent strings, and timestamp logs retained temporarily for active device session management and threat detection.

03 How We Use Your Information

Personal metadata collected by KvaultX is processed strictly for legitimate operational purposes:

  • To authenticate your identity during login, passkey verification, and multi-factor authentication.
  • To dispatch automated Security Login Alerts and Master Password Change Notifications whenever account activity is detected.
  • To send 6-digit Email OTP codes when performing multi-factor login or password reset workflows.
  • To detect and mitigate brute-force attacks, unauthorized credential manipulations, or suspicious active device sessions.

04 Security Login Alerts & Immediate Account Blocking Protocol

To prevent unauthorized account takeovers and credential abuse, KvaultX enforces an active Instant Threat Containment Protocol:

🚨 Instant Account Block Capability

Every automated security alert email contains a direct "🚨 Report & Block Account Immediately" button. If an intruder logs in or updates credentials using your email address, clicking this button instantly locks your account and revokes all active device sessions.

  • Immediate Session Termination: Triggering a security report destroys all active JWT authentication tokens across every device and browser globally.
  • Account Lockdown: The account status is set to `isBlocked: true`, rejecting subsequent login attempts until formal identity verification is completed.

05 Cookies & Session Token Policy

KvaultX utilizes modern web security tokens and strictly necessary cookies to keep you safely authenticated:

  • JWT Tokens: Short-lived Access Tokens and Refresh Tokens generated upon successful multi-factor login.
  • HttpOnly, SameSite=Lax Cookies: Secured session cookies that prevent Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) and Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) exploits.
  • No Tracking Cookies: We do NOT employ third-party tracking pixels, marketing cookies, or user tracking scripts.

06 Data Retention & Permanent Account Erasure

You retain full sovereignty over your vault contents at all times:

  • Immediate Deletion: Items deleted from your vault inside the KvaultX Dashboard are purged immediately from database records.
  • Account Termination: If you request full account deletion, your encrypted vault items, user profile, and active session histories are permanently purged without recovery options.

07 Third-Party Disclosure & Global Regulations

We do not trade, sell, or rent user data. KvaultX complies with global data privacy frameworks (including GDPR principles) ensuring your personal email and encrypted payload remain secure.
