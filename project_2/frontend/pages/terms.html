01 Zero-Knowledge Architecture & Master Password Responsibility

KvaultX operates strictly under a Zero-Knowledge Encryption Model. By using KvaultX, you acknowledge and agree to the following technical parameters:

  • Client-Side Encryption: Your master password and vault items are encrypted locally on your browser/device before reaching our network.
  • Zero Password Access: KvaultX never receives, stores, or logs your plain-text master password or vault encryption keys.
  • User Responsibility: You are solely responsible for memorizing and securing your master password. Because KvaultX operates zero-knowledge servers, lost master passwords cannot be recovered by KvaultX support staff.

06 Unauthorized Login & Password Change Security Protocols

For state-of-the-art security, KvaultX dispatches instant Security Notification Emails for every successful authentication attempt and master password update:

🚨 1-Click Report & Block Protocol

If an unauthorized user logs in or updates credentials using your personal email, every security email contains a direct "🚨 Report & Block Account Immediately" button. Clicking this link instantly revokes all active device sessions across all browsers and locks the account so the intruder cannot access your vault from any device.

  • Global Session Revocation: Triggering an unauthorized report immediately terminates all JWT session tokens on all active devices.
  • Lockout State: The account remains in a protected blocked state until formal identity verification is conducted with KvaultX Support.
